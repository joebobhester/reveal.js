BeneFlakes Cereal

A fictitious brand of breakfast cereal. Nationally distributed and health oriented. First introduced in 2009. Primary competitors are Kellogg's and General Mills' healthier cereals.

Situation Analysis

Through EOY 2013, sales are up 5 percent vs. the previous year.

4th year of positive sales growth, but rate of growth is slowing.

Marketing Objectives / Strategies

Increase BeneFlakes penetration among frequent cereal eaters in two ways:

Year-round focus on health benefits of cereals

Heavy-up during primary cold cereal months (spring/summer)

Advertising Plan

January - December 2014

$50 million working media budget

Advertising Objective

Raise awareness of the health benefits and good taste of BeneFlakes cereal among target from the current level of 35 percent to a goal of 40 percent during calendar year 2014.

Media Objectives

Target Audience Objective

Advertise to heavy or medium cereal eaters in the natural cereal category.

  • Women ages 18-49. HHI $75,000+
  • Well educated, prof/mgr, nutrition-oriented

The target consists of 19.5 million women who can be defined as natural cereal users. They represent 20 percent of all women.

Reach/Frequency Objective

Achieve the following average 4-week delivery:

  • Peak Period: 3+ Reach = 35
  • Rest of Year: 3+ Reach = 10

Seasonality Objective #1

Provide year-round media support to stimulate usage throughout year, with additional weight during the peak summer months.

Seasonality Objective #2

Schedule advertising to run Wednesday-Sunday to complement key grocery shopping days.

Geographic Objective

Provide national advertising support.

Media Strategies

Following success of the brand during the past four years, the 2014 media plan recommends a continuation of the media strategy, using television as the primary paid medium with select magazines as the secondary paid media type, along with paid search. BeneFlakes has enhanced its website to offer diet and nutrition information, as well as sponsoring The Biggest Loser program on TV. Its Facebook page provides contests and games related to those topics.

Television

As the primary medium, national TV provides:

  • High reach / awareness
  • Targeting ability (nich networks)
  • Immediacy of message
  • Continuity (lower cost cable nets)
  • Contextual relevance
  • Emotional connection to viewer

TV Programming / Nets

Prime-time TV shows that appeal to the target audience include sitcoms, dramas, and reality/news. The following cable networks are more likely to be viewed by the target:

  • Bravo
  • Biography
  • BBC America
  • HGTV
  • Travel Channel
  • MSNBC
  • Animal Planet
  • Bravo

As a smaller brand, BeneFlakes will include only a small amount of broadcast network TV during peak periods.

TV Dayparts

  • 50% Prime-time: provides greatest reach
  • 25% Weekend Daytime: offers suitable context (relaxed/shopping)
  • 25% Early morning: news-oriented context to promote health benefits.

Magazines

As the secondary medium, magazines provide:

  • Long message life
  • Repeat exposure
  • Targeting ability (contextually relevant and engaging content)
  • Editorial compatibility
  • Website linkage

Magazine Ads / Positions

Ads will include both hardcopy print (1P4C) and the tablet version wherever possible. The latter will have links to the brand's website and Facebook page, and allow the reader to interact with related dietary information.

Preferred Position:

  • Health & Fitness - Fitness section
  • Food - Front of book
  • General/Entertainment - News section

Recommended Mags (Index / Coverage)

  • Shape (140 / 6.7%)
  • Self (139 / 6.7%)
  • Real Simple (140 / 7.6%)
  • Family Fun (137 / 5.3%)
  • Food & Wine (133 / 5.1%)
  • Martha Stewart Living (132 / 12.0%)
  • Cooking Light (130 / 11.3%)

Internet: Paid Search

As the third medium, the Internet provides:

  • Response-based advertising
  • contextual relevance
  • Behavioral targeting

In addition, Google AdSense will be used to drive Search on natural health-related keywords.

Owned Media: Brand Website

BeneFlakes invested in a redesign of its website in 2012 to provide greater interactivity with its brands, as wel as links to other sites with diet and health information, including video clips from the TV show it sponsors, The Biggest Loser.

Sponsorship

For its connection to The Biggest Loser, BeneFlakes will get TV billboards before and during the show each week in addition to commercial time, and a significant presence on the TV show's website and in its app. It will include sponsored program-related quizzes and games in those venues where consumers can sigh up to receive weekly nutrition tips.

Earned Media: Social Networks

In addition to BeneFlakes' website, it has enhanced its Facebook page to encourage users to share their stories of how they incorporated BeneFlakes into their own diets and health plans.

Global State

Set data-state="something" on a slide and "something" will be added as a class to the document element when the slide is open. This lets you apply broader style changes, like switching the background.

Custom Events

Additionally custom events can be triggered on a per slide basis by binding to the data-state name. 


Reveal.addEventListener( 'customevent', function() {
	console.log( '"customevent" has fired' );
} );

Slide Backgrounds

Set data-background="#007777" on a slide to change the full page background to the given color. All CSS color formats are supported.

Image Backgrounds

<section data-background="image.png">

Repeated Image Backgrounds

<section data-background="image.png" data-background-repeat="repeat" data-background-size="100px">

Background Transitions

Pass reveal.js the backgroundTransition: 'slide' config argument to make backgrounds slide rather than fade.

Background Transition Override

You can override background transitions per slide by using data-background-transition="slide".
